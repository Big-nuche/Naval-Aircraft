E-2

The E-2 Hawkeye was the Navy's first dedicated Airborne Early Warning aircraft, originally designed in 1960, by Grumman, upadated variants are still in frontline service to this day. The primary variants used the the Navy have been the E-2A/B/C/D, with the E-2D being the variant currently in use today. The E-2's primary role in the fleet has been airborne early warning, command and control, surveillance of land and sea, control of air defense, or strike aircraft during offensive operations, and controlling search and rescue operations as its core abilities.
